OTTERSIDE - Health Disparity in Medical Research

OTTERSIDE

OTTERSIDE focuses on the health disparity in the medical research field in today’s society.

Health disparity is “health differences due to sociocultural, behavioral, environmental, and/or health care/system factors according to U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.

Through research, our team was able to find out that most of the medical research is skewed towards certain groups of the population, such as specific gender, ethnicity, or race, instead of accurately representing the entire population. We wanted to highlight the disparity and allow the general population and the researchers to be aware of this situation, influencing us to create OTTER SIDE.
